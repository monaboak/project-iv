2010

Apple released its next iPhone just over a year later. The iPhone 4 model was introduced on June 7th, 2010 and hit the stores a few weeks later on June 24th. Apple took this release as an opportunity to remodel the physical structure of the iPhone. They marketed it as the “Thinnest Smartphone Ever”. It was during this era that the FaceTime feature was introduced. Apple accomplished a great deal with this release- greatly elevating the iPhone brand. Retina display, 5 megapixel camera and HD video recording gave way for the infamous wave of iPhone photography that started after the iPhone 4’s release. This release included three storage options 8GB, 16GB and 32GB. This iPhone came with Apple’s latest operating system IOS4. IOS4 included 100 new features and 1500 new APIs for developers. The jump from the 3GS to the 4 was Apple’s biggest to date. “iPhone 4 is the biggest leap since the original iPhone,” said Steve Jobs, Apple’s CEO. “FaceTime video calling sets a new standard for mobile communication, and our new Retina display is the highest resolution display ever in a phone, with text looking like it does on a fine printed page. We have been dreaming about both of these breakthroughs for decades.” The iPhone 4 sold nearly 40 million units worldwide.
