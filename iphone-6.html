2014-2015

iPhone 6 & 6s (+plus)

In 2015, Apple released the iPhone 6s and iPhone 6s Plus. They branded the 6s as “The Most Advanced iPhone Ever”. The feature of Live Photos was introduced- brining still images to life by capturing a moment in motion. 3D Touch and a new A9 chip were also introduced. Apple’s senior vice president of Worldwide Marketing stated:

“These are the most advanced iPhones ever, with 7000 series aluminum, ion-strengthened glass, the new 64-bit A9 chip, 12-megapixel iSight and 5-megapixel FaceTime HD cameras, faster Touch ID, LTE and Wi-Fi. Customers are going to love them.”

In 2014, Apple introduced the feature of having two different sizes of the same model with the release of the iPhone 6 and 6 plus. The phone was released on September 20th, 2014. The phone featured 4.7 inch and 5.5 inch Retina HD displays, and a new thinner design.

“iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus are the biggest advancements in iPhone history,” said Tim Cook, Apple’s CEO. “The iPhone is the most loved smartphone in the world with the highest customer satisfaction in the industry and we are making it much better in every way. Only Apple can combine the best hardware, software and services at this unprecedented level and we think customers are going to love it.”

Between 2014 and 2015, Apple sold a little over 400 million units worldwide.
